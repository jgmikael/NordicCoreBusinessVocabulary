# EU Company Certificate Application Profile (based on NCBV Ontology)

**Status:** Draft • **Version:** 0.0.1 • **Date:** 2025-10-14

## 1. Scope and Purpose

This page documents a human‑readable description of the **EU Company Certificate (EUCC) Application Profile** and its alignment with the **Nordic Core Business Vocabulary (NCBV)** OWL Ontology (v0.0.5). The Application Profile (AP) in `WE BUILD EU Company Certificate.ttl` defines properties intended to describe the EUCC; the underlying conceptual model and semantics **must** derive from NCBV.

### Legal baseline
The EU Company Certificate is established by **Directive (EU) 2025/25**, Article **16b**, which requires Member State registers to issue an EU Company Certificate accepted across the Union as sufficient evidence, at the time of issuance, of incorporation and the listed company information. The Article enumerates which data items must appear in the certificate for limited liability companies and partnerships.

## 2. Explanatory Overview (non‑technical)

### 2.1 What is the EU Company Certificate?
The EUCC is a harmonised extract issued by national business registers and recognised by all Member States. It provides core company facts (e.g., legal name(s), legal form, registration and EUID, registered office, representatives, object/NACE, capital where applicable, status, website, and date of issue) to support reliable cross‑border administrative and business processes.

### 2.2 Why an Application Profile?
An **application profile** specifies how a general ontology (NCBV) is used for a concrete artefact (here: the EUCC). It selects relevant classes and properties, constrains their usage, and packages them for publication and interoperable exchange.

### 2.3 Relationship to NCBV
- **NCBV v0.0.5** provides the classes and properties for modelling legal entities, identifiers, representation, and related business concepts.
- The EUCC AP **should reuse** NCBV terms (IRIs) for properties and classes. If the AP introduces its own terms, they should be declared as `rdfs:subPropertyOf` or aligned to the corresponding NCBV terms.

## 6. Notes on Alignment (non‑normative guidance)
- To be strictly based on NCBV, each AP property **should** either:
  - **reuse** the NCBV property IRI directly, or
  - be declared as `rdfs:subPropertyOf` the appropriate NCBV property, and optionally constrain with SHACL.
- Domains and ranges **should** be inherited from NCBV or explicitly constrained in SHACL for the EUCC context.
- Where Article 16b introduces data items (e.g., **EUID**, **object/NACE**, **legal representatives with full date of birth**), the AP **should** point to the corresponding NCBV classes/properties and reference controlled vocabularies where applicable.
